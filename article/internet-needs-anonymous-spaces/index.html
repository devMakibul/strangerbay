Why the Internet Needs More Anonymous Spaces

Published on February 12, 2026 · StrangerBay Team

An argument for preserving anonymous communication on the internet as social media giants push for real-name policies and mandatory verification.

The Push for Identity Verification

There is a growing movement among governments and tech companies to eliminate online anonymity. Proposals for mandatory identity verification to access social media, laws requiring platforms to verify users' real identities, and corporate policies favoring "real name" usage are becoming increasingly common worldwide.

The arguments for these measures are understandable: reducing harassment, combating misinformation, and preventing abuse. But the consequence of eliminating anonymous spaces online would be devastating for free expression.

Who Benefits from Anonymity

The people who benefit most from online anonymity are often the most vulnerable. LGBTQ+ individuals in countries where their identity is criminalized use anonymous platforms to find community and support. Whistleblowers use anonymous channels to expose corporate and government wrongdoing. Domestic abuse survivors use anonymous communication to seek help without alerting their abusers.

Political dissidents, journalists in hostile nations, activists organizing against authoritarian regimes, and just regular people who want to speak freely about unpopular opinions — all of these groups rely on the ability to communicate without their real identity being attached to their words.

The Chilling Effect of Surveillance

When people know they are being watched, they change their behavior. This is the "chilling effect" — a well-documented phenomenon where the awareness of surveillance leads to self-censorship even when no actual censorship is taking place. Studies show that government surveillance programs significantly reduce Google searches for sensitive topics, even in democratic countries.

A world without anonymous spaces is a world where people only say what they think is acceptable. And in a society that is changing rapidly, the ideas that are "unacceptable" today might be essential tomorrow.

The StrangerBay Philosophy

StrangerBay exists because we believe that anonymous communication is worth preserving. Not because anonymity is always used perfectly — it isn't. But because the alternative — a world where every word is permanently attached to your identity — is far more dangerous to individual freedom and authentic self-expression than the risks of anonymity.
